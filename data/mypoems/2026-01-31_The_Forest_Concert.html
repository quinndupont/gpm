The Forest Concert

A hamster named Hux had a piccolo thin—

so skinny the notes could barely fit in.

He blew and he blew till his whiskers went *fwee!*

and out came a sound like a hiccuping bee.


*Da-dump bump bump,

da-dump bump bump,*

the birches bent down

and the willows went wump!


A badger named Bix had a piano so wide

it took fourteen beetles to push it outside.

She crashed on the keys with her paws and her nose

and out came a sound like a thundering rose.


*Da-dump bump bump,

da-dump bump bump,*

the aspens did twirls

and the oak trees went thump!


A frog (who had no sort of instrument yet)

just opened his mouth wide as wide as it’d get—

he sang out a note like a bell made of rain,

and every tree leaned in to hear it again.


*Da-dump bump bump,

da-dump bump bump,*

the maples bowed low

and the pines gave a jump!


The piccolo whistled, the piano went CRASH,

the frog held his note and the forest went—


hush.


The sycamores swiveled, the cedars did sway,

the elms did a thing elms won’t speak of today.


*Da-dump bump bump,

da-dump bump bump—*


And then?


It got quiet.


The trees held their breath.


Hux lowered his piccolo, thin as a thread.

Bix lifted her paws. The frog blinked and said:


“That sound that we made when we all played together—

where does it go? Does it last? Is it never?”


The forest said nothing.

The wind didn’t know.

The music had ended.


But where did it go?


*Da-dump…*


*(bump)*
